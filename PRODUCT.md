# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Freedirect serves privacy-conscious Safari users on iPhone, iPad, and Mac who want links to large platforms to open in healthier alternative frontends without babysitting browser settings. They are often configuring the tool in short sessions, then expecting redirects to happen invisibly while browsing.

## Product Purpose

Freedirect is a minimal companion app plus Safari Web Extension that manages privacy-preserving URL redirects, instance choices, bypass controls, diagnostics, and backups in the extension's web UI. Success means the user can enable the extension, choose services and frontends, and trust that redirects either work or explain why they cannot.

## Brand Personality

Calm, exacting, protective. The interface should feel like a trustworthy system utility: quiet until needed, precise when explaining a redirect or limitation, and confident without sounding alarmist.

## Anti-references

Do not copy LibRedirect's interface or source. Avoid hacker-terminal styling, fear-based privacy marketing, dense preference panes with no onboarding, generic SaaS card grids, decorative glassmorphism, and UI that hides Safari permission constraints behind vague errors.

## Design Principles

1. Make Safari's permission model legible before users hit a dead end.
2. Treat redirect coverage as a verifiable system, not a promise: expose status, diagnostics, and limitations.
3. Prefer native platform patterns so the app feels at home on iOS, iPadOS, and macOS.
4. Keep routine configuration calm and compact; reserve emphasis for failures, unsafe choices, and first-run activation.
5. Build trust through transparent research notes, test evidence, and reversible settings.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA or better. Support VoiceOver-friendly labels, keyboard navigation on macOS/iPadOS, reduced-motion alternatives, non-color-only status indicators, and sufficient contrast for long settings sessions.
